Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is one of the most important historical monuments in India, located in the heart of Delhi.

It was built in 1648 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan and served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years.

Constructed using red sandstone, the fort is famous for its massive walls, intricate carvings, and impressive Mughal architecture.

The Red Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of India’s independence. Every year, the Prime Minister hoists the national flag here on Independence Day.

Inside the fort, visitors can explore palaces, halls, and museums that showcase India’s rich history.

The best time to visit the Red Fort is from October to March, and the evening light and sound show is a major attraction.
